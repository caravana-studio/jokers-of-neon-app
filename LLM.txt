JOKERS OF NEON - GAME MANUAL

GAME OBJECTIVE
- Build a stronger deck and clear rounds by reaching target score thresholds.
- Navigate map paths (rounds, shops, rage nodes) and adapt your strategy each run.
- In the meta game, earn XP, unlock season rewards, and compete in leaderboards and tournaments.

CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
- Start a run with limited hands and discards each round.
- Play poker hands to score points x multiplier.
- If you hit the target score, you clear the node and progress.
- If you fail with no hands left, the run ends.
- After progression nodes, visit shops to improve deck power, economy, and consistency.

POKER HAND TYPES
- Royal Flush: the highest-ranking straight flush.
- Straight Flush: five consecutive cards of the same suit.
- Five of a Kind: five cards of the same rank.
- Four of a Kind: four cards of the same rank.
- Full House: three of a kind plus a pair.
- Straight: five consecutive cards.
- Flush: five cards of the same suit.
- Three of a Kind: three cards of the same rank.
- Two Pair: two different pairs.
- Pair: two cards of the same rank.
- High Card: no combination; only highest card value applies.

SCORING BASICS
- A play score is calculated as: Points x Multi.
- Each hand belongs to a rarity category (C, B, A, S, SS).
- That category defines base values and per-level growth.

BASE POINTS AND MULTI BY PLAY
- SS | Royal Flush | 120 points | +9 multi | The highest-ranking hand with Ace, King, Queen, Jack, and Ten of the same suit.
- S | Straight Flush | 100 points | +8 multi | Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
- SS | Five of a Kind | 80 points | +8 multi | Five cards of the same rank.
- S | Four of a Kind | 60 points | +7 multi | Four cards of the same rank.
- A | Full House | 40 points | +4 multi | Three of a kind plus a pair.
- A | Flush | 35 points | +4 multi | Five cards of the same suit, not in sequence.
- A | Straight | 30 points | +4 multi | Five consecutive cards of different suits.
- B | Three of a Kind | 30 points | +3 multi | Three cards of the same rank.
- B | Two Pair | 20 points | +3 multi | Two different pairs of cards.
- C | Pair | 10 points | +2 multi | Two cards of the same rank.
- C | High Card | 5 points | +1 multi | The highest single card when no other hand is made.

LEVEL UP GROWTH (BY CATEGORY)
- SS: +35 points and +3 multi per level.
- S: +30 points and +2 multi per level.
- A: +25 points and +2 multi per level.
- B: +20 points and +1 multi per level.
- C: +10 points and +1 multi per level.

CARD SCORING NOTES
- Traditional cards follow face value (2-10, J/Q/K=10, A=11).
- Neon cards are upgraded variants and generally add +1 multi.
- Jokers and Wildcards help complete hands.
- Specials, Modifiers, Power-ups, and Rage effects can heavily change final round results.

CARD IMAGE RULES

CARD TYPES
- Traditional cards: ids 0-53 (includes Joker 52 and Wildcard 53).
- Neon cards: ids 200-253 (neon variants + neon joker/wild).
- Modifier cards: ids 600-699.
- Power-ups: ids 800-899 (single-use boosts).
- Special cards: mainly 10000-19999.
- Rage cards: ids 20000-29999.
- Loot boxes: ids 1-13.

SPECIAL CARD ID FORMAT (5 DIGITS)
- Pattern: 1SSCC
- Digit 1: always 1 (special-card namespace).
- Digits 2-3 (SS): season number (ascending from 01) or collection number (descending from 99).
- Examples: 01=Season 1, 02=Season 2, 99=GG collection.
- Digits 4-5 (CC): card id inside that season/collection.
- Examples: 10101 (S1 card 01), 10214 (S2 card 14), 19903 (GG card 03).

RARITY TIERS
- C=Common, B=Rare, A=Epic, S=Legendary, SS=Mythic.

ECONOMY (IN-GAME VALUES)

- Loot box shop values (sell): C:750, B:1500, A:2000, S:0, SS:0
- Power-up base shop costs (buy): B:100, A:200, S:350, SS:500
- Power-up shop values (sell): B:50, A:50, S:100, SS:150

META GAME
- Seasons last approximately 6 weeks.
- A season-end tournament runs for approximately 10 days.
- Season progression:
  - Free + Premium (Season Pass) reward track.
  - Includes packs and tournament entries.
  - Progress is XP-driven with milestone rewards.
- XP sources:
  - Daily missions.
  - Weekly missions.
  - Level clears.
  - Mission XP is read from the active on-chain mission slot; do not assume fixed XP by difficulty.
- Daily and weekly missions:
  - Include score, deck-building, rage, poker-hand, economy, shop, power-up, burn, free-pack, and run-completion goals.
  - Daily missions reset around 06:00 UTC by default.
  - Weekly missions reset with the game leaderboard week.
- Season Pass benefits:
  - Unlocks premium reward lane.
  - Increases max lives from 3 to 6.
  - Improves life recharge from every 8h to every 4h.
  - Includes a 20% season-pack discount.
- Lives:
  - One life is spent per new run.
  - Lives refill over time up to your cap.
- Collections and skins:
  - Each season introduces exclusive special cards.
  - Collection grouping aligns with special-card SS digits.
  - Skins are alternate visuals with their own rarity tiers.

SEASON PASS, LIVES, AND DAILY CADENCE
- Without Season Pass: max 3 lives, recharge every 8 hours.
- With Season Pass: max 6 lives, recharge every 4 hours.
- Daily and weekly missions are primary repeatable XP sources; level clears add additional XP.

MAP AND NODE FLOW
- Main node families: Round, Store, Rage (plus reward/other utility nodes).
- Rounds are regular scoring challenges.
- Stores are upgrade/economy nodes with different inventory profiles.
- Rage nodes are challenge spikes with temporary debuffs and higher tension.

RAGE ROUNDS
- Rage rounds apply one or more rage effects for that round.
- Rage effects include suit silences, hand-size penalties, discard bans, hand restrictions, etc.
- Rage nodes can be intermediate/final/final boss style milestones.
- Boss-level milestone exists in progression flow (commonly represented as level 3).

MAP STRUCTURE
- Node types: Round, Store, Rage.
- Map generation per level:
  - Total stages = 4 + (level * 1). Level 1 = 5 stages, Level 2 = 6, Level 3 = 7.
  - Stage type distribution: 90% Round, 10% Rage (randomly distributed).
  - Store nodes appear after every stage node (100% probability).
  - Maximum 4 nodes per stage; each additional node beyond the first has a 50% chance.
  - First stage is always a Round. Last stage is always a boss Rage.
  - Minimum 2 rages per map, maximum 5 (excluding the final boss rage).
  - Rages are spaced roughly evenly across the map.
- Node graph:
  - Nodes in each stage connect to nodes in the next stage.
  - Each node in the next stage has at least 1 guaranteed parent edge.
  - Additional edges have 20% probability each.
  - Players choose their path through branching nodes.

RAGE ROUND POWER SYSTEM
- Each rage node has a power budget that determines how many and how strong the rage cards are.
- Non-final rages: power = min(rage_encounter_count, 8). First rage = power 1, second = power 2, etc.
- Final boss rage: power = min(rage_encounter_count, 8) + 2. Always the strongest.
- Rage card selection algorithm:
  - Each rage rarity tier has a power cost (C=1, B=2, A=3, S=4).
  - The system randomly selects rage cards until the total power budget is spent.
  - Probability per tier: C=45%, B=25%, A=20%, S=10%.
  - Each rage card can appear only once per node.
  - If total power from all available rages is less than or equal to the budget, all rages are used.
- Rage level reduction:
  - Players accumulate a rage level from previously defeated rages.
  - If rage_level >= rage_node.round, the effective round for score calculation is reduced to max(1, round - rage_level).
  - This makes later rages slightly easier by lowering the target score.

RAGE ROUND CONFIGURATION
- Initial rage probability: 35% (chance of random rage node in non-guaranteed slots)
- Probability increment per round: +15%
- Rages quantity threshold for extra rounds: 6
- Maximum active rages: 4
- Minimum round level before rage activation: 3
- Level cooldown: 1

LEVELS AND ROUNDS STRUCTURE
- A run is divided into levels. Each level generates a procedural map with multiple stages.
- The number of stages per level grows as you progress: total_stages = 4 + (level * 1).
  - Level 1: 5 stages, Level 2: 6 stages, Level 3: 7 stages, etc.
- Stages can be Round nodes, Rage nodes, or Store nodes, determined by probability during map generation.
  - Round nodes: 90% base probability. These are combat encounters where the player must reach a target score.
  - Rage nodes: 10% base probability, plus forced rage nodes placed evenly across the map (minimum 2, maximum 5 per level). The last stage of every level is always a Rage boss node.
  - Store nodes: inserted after other nodes with 100% probability (if the player has unlocked shop types).
- The number of rounds per level is NOT fixed — it depends on the procedurally generated map layout.
- The round counter is cumulative across the entire run (it never resets between levels). This counter determines the target score for each round.
- A level is completed when the player beats the final node (Rage boss) of the level map. Upon level completion, the player advances to the next level and a new map is generated.

ROGUELIKE SHOP-TIER PROGRESSION
- The roguelike progression layer unlocks shop inventory/features over repeated runs through the shop tier system.
- The frontend reads player progression from `shop_views.get_player_tier(player)`, normalized as player tier plus optional total runs, max level, and max round.
- The frontend reads the ordered unlock ladder from `shop_views.get_unlock_list()`. Entries normalize to order, unlockId, optional required runs, max level, max round, and shop type.
- Modifiers are treated as baseline-unlocked for shop visibility.
- Store node/tooltips filter visible item families from current player tier. Known shop item families are traditional/neon cards, modifiers, specials, power-ups, level-ups, loot boxes, and burn.
- `TierUnlockedEvent` is parsed from play responses as `ShopTierUnlockedEvent { game_id, unlock_id }`.
- The play-event parser supports multiple `TierUnlockedEvent` entries in a single response (`shopTierUnlockedEvents[]`). There is no frontend cap of two unlocks: if several tiers unlock at once, they are stored as a queue.
- The legacy single `shopTierUnlockedEvent` field remains for compatibility and points to the first parsed unlock.
- On game over, Summary checks the queued unlocks. If at least one valid unlock config exists, it routes to `/shop-tier-unlocked/:gameId`; otherwise it routes to the normal game-over screen.
- The unlock screen filters invalid unlock ids, shows one unlocked item at a time, and uses a NEXT button until the queue is exhausted. It clears the queue only when the player leaves the unlock flow or starts another run.
- Known unlock ids configured in the frontend: specials_c, powerups_cb, slot_2, play_4, specials_b, slot_3, modifiers_ba, powerups_as, slot_4, discard_4, levelups, specials_a, slot_5, traditionals, play_5, lootboxes, neon, slot_6, burn, discard_5, specials_s, slot_7.
- Unlock effects include availability of special-card rarities, power-up rarities, modifier rarities, traditional cards, neon cards, loot boxes, level-ups, burn, higher special-card slot caps, and higher starting plays/discards.
- Legacy numeric tier ids are still mapped to unlock ids where possible, but new flows should prefer string unlock ids from the event/list.

ROUND TARGET SCORE CURVE
- The round number used for score calculation is a global counter that increments across all levels in a run.
- Formula (from calculate_round_score in game_config):
  - Rounds 1-5: 300 * round => 300 / 600 / 900 / 1,200 / 1,500
  - Rounds 6-11: 2,400 + (round - 6) * 900 => 2,400 / 3,300 / 4,200 / 5,100 / 6,000 / 6,900
  - Rounds 12-18: 10,500 + (round - 12) * 3,600 => 10,500 / 14,100 / 17,700 / 21,300 / 24,900 / 28,500 / 32,100
  - Rounds 19-26: 50,100 + (round - 19) * 18,000 => 50,100 / 68,100 / 86,100 / ... / 176,100
  - Rounds 27-35: 284,100 + (round - 27) * 108,000
  - Rounds 36-45: 1,904,100 + (round - 36) * 756,000
  - Rounds 46-56: 14,756,100 + (round - 46) * 6,048,000
  - Rounds 57+: 178,052,100 + (round - 57) * 54,432,000
- Growth multiplier per tier: ~3x, ~4x, ~5x, ~6x, ~7x, ~8x, ~9x (exponential acceleration).

DISCOUNT SYSTEM

- Max discounts per shop visit: 3

  - No discount: 70%

  - A-Grade (30% off): 10%
  - S-Grade (50% off): 5%

MISSIONS (DAILY AND WEEKLY)
- Daily and weekly missions are served by `daily_missions_system`.
- Period ids: daily=1, weekly=2. Difficulty ids: easy=1, medium=2, hard=3.
- Current daily missions are read with `get_today_missions`; current weekly missions are read with `get_this_week_missions`.
- Mission slots normalize from 9 fields: period_type, period_id, difficulty, mission_id, template_id, target, param_1, param_2, xp.
- Player progress is read with `get_player_mission_progress(player, period_type, period_id)` for both periods.
- Daily in-game progress can also read `get_game_daily_mission_progress(game_id)` so the Missions page can show live per-run progress.
- Daily missions reset at `VITE_RESET_TIME_UTC` (default 06:00 UTC). Weekly missions reset at the next weekly leaderboard reset, using the Monday-to-Sunday UTC game week.
- The Home missions banner has Daily/Weekly tabs and a reset countdown. The Missions page shows weekly and daily panels; in-game mode shows daily progress bars.
- Completion toasts support weekly and daily titles from the mission `period_type`; legacy `DailyMissionXPEvent` is still parsed as daily.
- Mission descriptions are rendered from `missionTemplates` translations. `target` renders as quantity, score, or level. `param_1` can select a poker hand or suit, and `param_2` is used for the hand in `daily-flush-suit`.
- Daily templates: daily-play-hand, daily-reach-level, daily-add-trad, daily-add-mod, daily-add-neon, daily-flush-suit, daily-deck-size, daily-deck-neon, daily-deck-jokers, daily-deck-neon-jokers, daily-round-no-discards, daily-all-neon-hand, daily-play-hand-count, daily-score-single, daily-deck-neon-suit, daily-deck-wild, daily-add-suit, daily-use-power-ups, daily-sell-power-ups, daily-burn-cards, daily-wild-joker-hands, daily-level-up-hand.
- Weekly templates: weekly-score, weekly-lootboxes, weekly-specials, weekly-free-packs, weekly-daily-done, weekly-play-hand, weekly-defeat-rages, weekly-burn-cards, weekly-sell-specials, weekly-win-rounds, weekly-win-levels, weekly-round-no-discards, weekly-burn-neon, weekly-burn-modifier, weekly-buy-trad, weekly-buy-neon-joker, weekly-neon-hands, weekly-use-power-ups, weekly-rerolls, weekly-special-slots, weekly-sell-power-ups, weekly-complete-runs, weekly-win-rerolls, weekly-level-up-hand.
